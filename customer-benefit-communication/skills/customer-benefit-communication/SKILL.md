# Customer Benefit Communication

Transform technical, feature-focused customer communication into benefit-driven
messages. Customers are typically **non-technical decision-makers** — they care
about what your product or service does *for them*, not how it works.

Every piece of communication must answer: **"What does this mean for me and my business?"**

## Workflow

1. **Detect** — Identify channel, recipient role, and lifecycle stage.
   Read [assets/step-1-detect.md](assets/step-1-detect.md) for classification tables.

2. **Analyze** — Scan for benefit gaps (feature-only statements, jargon, missing outcomes).
   Read [assets/step-2-analyze.md](assets/step-2-analyze.md) for red/green flag patterns.

3. **Research** — If the communication mentions specific products or services, read
   [assets/product-knowledge.md](assets/product-knowledge.md) for product knowledge and
   benefit framing starters. Then **use web search** to verify current features,
   pricing, and capabilities before making specific product claims.
   Product info changes frequently — never rely solely on cached knowledge.

4. **Rewrite** — Apply the Benefit Bridge Formula and tone adaptation.
   Read [assets/step-3-rewrite.md](assets/step-3-rewrite.md) for the formula, examples, and tone guide.

Deliver output as: **A) Brief gap analysis → B) Rewritten communication ready to send.**

Match the input language. Use "you/your" (or local equivalent) — make the customer the subject.

## Company Context

See [assets/company-profile.md](assets/company-profile.md) for the company's profile,
differentiators, products, and customer proof points. Use as background context
when writing — never recite it directly to the customer.

## Gotchas

- **Never invent metrics.** Only use numbers the company has verified and approved.
- **Don't oversell.** Honest, understated benefit beats inflated claims every time.
- **"Benefit" ≠ only money.** Peace of mind and time saved often matter more than cost.
- **Customers are not technical.** Translate everything into plain language.
- **Match the scope.** A support reply needs one benefit sentence, not three paragraphs.
- **Respect the original intent.** Enhance with benefit framing — don't change the message.
- **Match the input language.** If the original is Danish, reply in Danish. If English, English.
