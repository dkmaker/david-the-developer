# Step 2: Analyze for Benefit Gaps

## Red Flags (benefit is missing)

| Pattern | Example | Problem |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Feature-only** | "We installed X" / "We updated Y" | Missing: what the customer gains |
| **Jargon without translation** | "MFA", "Azure AD", "endpoint protection" | Missing: plain-language impact |
| **Internal process language** | "Patch cycle completed", "Maintenance done" | Missing: why the customer should care |
| **Passive/generic value** | "This improves security" | Missing: concrete, specific benefit |
| **No recipient awareness** | Same text to CEO and end-user | Missing: tailored relevance |

## Green Flags (benefit is present)

- ✅ Connects action to customer outcome: "…so your team can…"
- ✅ Quantifies impact: "…saves you approximately…"
- ✅ References customer's context: "…for your 30 employees…"
- ✅ Uses the customer's language, not technical jargon
- ✅ Makes the customer the subject ("You get…", "Your team…")

## Benefit Categories

Choose based on what matters most to the recipient:

### 1. Operational
Fewer disruptions, faster help, smooth onboarding, seamless remote work.

### 2. Financial
Predictable costs, no surprise invoices, no hardware investment, time = money.

### 3. Strategic
Future-proof systems, compliance (GDPR, NIS2, ISO 27001, etc.), scalability.

### 4. Peace of Mind
24/7 monitoring, safe data, "you don't need to understand the tech — that's our job."
